Tribute to Daniel Toroitich Arap Moi

Introduction

Daniel Toroitich Arap Moi was the second President of Kenya, serving from 1978 to 2002. He was a significant figure in Kenyan politics and played a crucial role in the country's development during his tenure.

Early Life

Born on September 2, 1924, in Sacho, Baringo County, Moi grew up in a humble background. He trained as a teacher and later ventured into politics, where he made a lasting impact.

Political Career

Moi became Vice President in 1967 and assumed the presidency in 1978 following the death of President Jomo Kenyatta. His leadership was marked by efforts to promote unity and development in Kenya.

Legacy

Moi's legacy includes the introduction of the 8-4-4 education system and various infrastructure projects. He is remembered for his commitment to peace and stability in Kenya and the region.
